Who is The Weeknd?

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye, professionally known as The Weeknd, is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer. He was born on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, to Ethiopian immigrant parents. The Weeknd’s parents divorced when he was young, and he grew up primarily with his grandmother and mother.

The Weeknd experimented with drugs and alcohol and dropped out of high school during his teenage years. He later credited his experiences with substance abuse as a significant influence on his music, stating that it helped him find his voice and express his emotions.

In 2010, The Weeknd anonymously uploaded several songs to YouTube and later released three mixtapes: “House of Balloons,” “Thursday,” and “Echoes of Silence.” These mixtapes gained significant critical acclaim and helped launch The Weeknd’s career. In 2012, he signed with Republic Records and released his debut album, “Trilogy,” which compiled the songs from his three mixtapes.

The Weeknd’s second album, “Kiss Land,” was released in 2013 and included the hit single “Live For” featuring Drake. In 2014, he contributed two songs to the “Fifty Shades of Grey” soundtrack, including the popular hit “Earned It.” The following year, The Weeknd released his third album, “Beauty Behind the Madness,” which included the chart-topping singles “Can’t Feel My Face” and “The Hills.”

The album earned him two Grammy Awards and established him as a mainstream pop star. In addition to his music career, The Weeknd has also made several appearances in film and television.

He made his acting debut in the 2019 film “Uncut Gems,” starring Adam Sandler, and has also appeared in episodes of the TV shows “American Dad!” and “Robot Chicken.” The Weeknd’s journey from an anonymous artist to a global pop star is a testament to his unique talent and ability to connect with audiences through his music. His success has cemented his place as one of the most influential and innovative artists of his generation.
