Lainey Wilson is an American country music singer-songwriter. Wilson performed from an early age, before going to Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue a career as a country performer.

Wilson was born and raised in the little Louisiana hamlet of Baskin, population 250. Her mother Michelle worked as a teacher, and her father Brian was a farmer. She developed an early interest in music.

Her family frequently listened to Glen Campbell and Buck Owens’ traditional country music. For my family and I, country music was more than just music. We actually lived those songs’ lyrics, she told The Advocate.

She first became interested in country music when she was nine years old and went to see the Grand Ole Opry. Wilson’s father gave her a few chords to learn, and by the time she was a preteen, she had started writing songs.

She published an extended play (EP) named Country Girls Rule on Myspace in 2006. Wilson worked as a Hannah Montana impersonator in high school.

She frequently organized her own performances, appearing as Hannah Montana at fairs, festivals, and birthday celebrations all around Louisiana, Mississippi, and Arkansas. Once, she even entertained young cancer patients at St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital.

In August 2011, Wilson moved to Nashville, Tennessee, after completing high school. She initially made her home in a camper trailer next to a Nashville recording facility. Wilson’s water and electricity were paid for by the studio owner in an effort to make ends meet.

Wilson acknowledged in 2021 that her first years in Nashville were challenging, saying, “It taught me that this thing was not going to be simple. It helped me develop persistence.

Wilson performed at numerous small venues for several years while honing her songwriting. Wilson put out a self-titled album on the Cupit label in 2014. She released her second album, Tougher, in 2016. The Lone Chief record company issued the album.

At the 56th Annual Country Music Association Awards, Wilson garnered the most nominations with six, making him the fourth artist to do so as a first-time nominee. In 2022, Wilson also became a member of the Yellowstone cast.
