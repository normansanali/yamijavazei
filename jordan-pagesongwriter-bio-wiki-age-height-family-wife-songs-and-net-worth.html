Jordan Page Biography

Jordan Page is an American singer-songwriter and musician who was born on 13 June 1979 in the United States. He started writing music with a political bent when Bush was the president, in December 2006. Some of his early music is critical of United States foreign policy. Jordan has appeared on CSPAN as a performer and has also hosted the show known as Page Against The Machine on Liberty Movement Radio. He wrote a song known as Song for Bob Dylan, which looks at the power of music in spurring social change.

Jordan Songwriter

Jordan has focused his career on creating music similar in style and purpose to late-20th century folk musicians before him. His song Song for Bob Dylan picks out some of the similarities between himself and other musicians who have sought political change through music. The song clearly mentions his and Bob Dylan’s shared view that war is evil

Jordan has followed Congressman Ron Paul since late 2007 and began writing songs about the opinions of the congressman. He also performed in support of Pat McGeehan’s campaign for a seat in the West Virginia Senate and at events correlated with the Free State Project, such as the Porcupine Freedom Festival.
