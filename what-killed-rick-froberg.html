Rick Froberg Death: What Killed Rick Froberg?: Rick Froberg was an American musician and visual artist.

He developed a passion for music and arts at a younger age, was consistent throughout his career, and rose through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after American acts

As a musician, he was the singer and guitarist for the San Diego-area bands Pitchfork, Drive Like Jehu, and Hot Snakes, performing with fellow San Diego musician John Reis.

As a visual artist, he created album art, promotional artwork, and merchandise designs for all of his bands as well as for Reis’ band Rocket from the Crypt and Swami Records label.

In 2022, the Trash Lamb Gallery in San Diego’s South Park featured an exhibition of posters Froberg created for the area’s underground music scene titled “Let My People Go: Art and Illustration 1988 to Present.”

He was also known by the pseudonyms Rick Fork and Rick Farr and performed on more than 20 albums. His last completed album, “Jericho Sirens” with Hot Snakes, was released in 2018.

Sadly, the frontman for Drive Like Jehu and Hot Snakes has died. Rick Froberg passed away at 55 on Friday, June 30, 2023, due to an undiagnosed heart condition. His death was confirmed in a post via his official Instagram account.

“it’s with raw shock and a shattered heart that i share news here of Rick’s sudden death, due to an undiagnosed heart condition. as his long-time friend and current sweetie, i’m relieved to tell you that he went quickly and did not suffer a lingering end —a thing that would suit him not. fast, savage, and without bullshit; such as he fancied in life.
