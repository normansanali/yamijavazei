Career

Bebe Rexha started her career as a songwriter and singer after she signed a contract with a talent scout named Samantha Cox. After that, she started working with popular American musician and songwriter Pete Wentz and also became a member of an electronic music band named Black Cards formed by Wentz.

In the year 2013, she signed a contract with Warner Bros. Records and wrote several popular songs Like a Champion, The Monster, Take Me Home, and Glowing. In the year 2014, she released her debut single titled I Can’t Stop Drinking About You along with a music video.

In the same year, she has also been featured in the popular song This Is Not a Drill by popular rapper Pitbull. She has also performed on NBC’s Today show and was picked as Artist of the Month by Elvis Duran. Later in the same year, she has released two more singles titled I’m Gonna Show You Crazy and Gone. In the next year, she has released her debut extended play titled I Don’t Wanna Grow Up. She also co-wrote the popular song titled Hey Mama along with Nicki Minaj and Afrojack.

In the year 2015, she has also co-written and featured in a song titled Me, Myself & I by G-Eazy. In the year 2016, she has also released a single titled No Broken Hearts and also released the music video of that song featuring Nicki Minaj. Later in the same year, she released another song titled I Got You and released an extended play titled All Your Fault.

In the year 2017, she started a world tour named All Your Fault Tour to promote her extended play across North America and Europe. In the same year, a documentary titled Bebe Rexha The Ride was aired on MTV and she has worked as a featured artist in the song titled Back to You along with Louis Tomlinson.
