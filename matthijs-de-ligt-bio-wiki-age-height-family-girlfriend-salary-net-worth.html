Matthijs De Ligt Football

Ligt is a vastly recognized professional footballer. Currently, he plays as a center-back for Serie A club Juventus. Likewise, he plays the Netherlands national team. It is known that he made his debut for Ajax’s senior team in a cup game against Willem II in 2016.

While here, Matthijs scored from a corner after 25 minutes. Citing this, he became the second-youngest goalscorer ever behind Clarence Seedorf. Afterward, he became the youngest player ever to play in a major European final.

Ligt began his career against Manchester United in the 2017 UEFA Europa League Final. Here, he won the Golden Boy award on 17 December 2018. As a result, he became the first defender to win the award.

Most noteworthy, Matthijs’ performances at Ajax earned him a move to Serie A side Juventus. In addition, the experience saw him win the Kopa Trophy in 2019. Citing this, he won the Serie A title during his first season with the Turin side.

Furthermore, De Ligt made his debut for the Dutch national team in a 2018 FIFA World Cup qualification match. He started and played 90 minutes during the UEFA Nations League.
